# Toronto 2047 — Megacorporations

## Overview

Five megacorporations hold Corporate Sovereignty rights in Toronto under the 2031 Act. Each controls specific sectors, industries, and zones within the city. They compete, cooperate, spy on each other, and occasionally wage proxy wars through hired crews.

---

## 1. Nexus Corp

**Tagline:** *"Life, Optimized."*
**Sector:** Biotechnology, Pharmaceutical, Genetic Engineering
**CEO:** Dr. Yuen Zhao-Harrington (Age 61, augmented to apparent 40)
**HQ:** Nexus Tower, The Spire District — 94 floors, floors 60-94 restricted

### What They Do
Nexus Corp is the dominant biotech and pharmaceutical megacorp in the Toronto Sovereignty Zone. They control 70% of prescription drug distribution, run twelve private medical clinics (access requires a Nexus account), and hold patents on 340 genetic therapies developed from data harvested through their "wellness programs."

Their most controversial division is **BioSynth Labs**, which conducts human augmentation trials on consenting (and allegedly non-consenting) subjects. The BioSynth facility is located on sub-levels B4 through B9 of Nexus Tower — access requires Tier-5 clearance.

### Key Assets
- **GenVault**: An encrypted biological data repository containing genomic profiles of 8 million Toronto residents collected through wellness programs
- **NexusMed Clinics**: 12 facilities citywide, each with biometric entry and Vantage-linked surveillance
- **Project LAZARUS**: Classified research into cellular regeneration and extended lifespan — rumored to have achieved 200+ year life extension in animal trials
- **Nexus Razors**: Elite corporate security, ex-military, heavily augmented with Helix-manufactured chrome

### Security Profile
- **External**: Biometric gates, Vantage-linked cameras, armed Razor patrols (3-person fire teams)
- **Tower Floors 1-59**: Commercial/office, Tier-1 to Tier-3 clearance, security density moderate
- **Tower Floors 60-94**: R&D and executive, Tier-4 to Tier-5 clearance, AI-assisted threat detection
- **BioSynth Sub-levels**: Independent air-gapped network, human guard rotation every 4 hours, pressure-sensor flooring
- **Known Weakness**: Nexus's security AI (designation: ARGUS-3) has a documented 11-second reboot window during shift change at 03:00 local time

### Relationships
- **Axiom Systems**: Tense détente — Axiom provides Nexus's security hardware; Nexus provides Axiom's medical support
- **Helix Dynamics**: Direct rivals in augmentation market; ongoing patent disputes
- **Vantage Data**: Paying client — Nexus buys behavioral profiles for targeted pharmaceutical marketing
- **OmniCore Energy**: Standard commercial relationship; OmniCore powers the Tower

### Vulnerability Intel
- Chief of Security **Marcus Okafor** has a known gambling debt to the Silk Network — potential leverage
- Three BioSynth researchers have made contact with the Collective; one (Dr. Priya Nair, Badge ID NX-4471) is suspected of leaking internal memos
- GenVault's backup uplink runs through the Digital Quarter's node cluster — intercept possible without entering the Tower

## 2. Axiom Systems

**Tagline:** *"Security Without Compromise."*
**Sector:** Military Technology, AI Warfare Systems, Private Security Services
**CEO:** General (Ret.) Dax Vortmann (Age 55, full combat-spec augmentation)
**HQ:** Axiom Citadel, The Fringe District — fortified compound, not a tower

### What They Do
Axiom Systems is the city's primary military-industrial contractor. They manufacture combat drones, AI targeting systems, neural-interface weapons, and the security hardware used by every major corporate security force in Toronto — including their rivals. This gives Axiom leverage over everyone.

Their **Phantom Division** is an open secret: a black-ops unit that conducts corporate espionage, targeted elimination, and infrastructure sabotage on behalf of paying clients. If a competitor's server farm burns down, check Phantom Division's schedule.

### Key Assets
- **AEGIS Network**: A city-wide threat-assessment AI that processes security feeds, predicts insurgent activity, and autonomously deploys drone response
- **Axiom Citadel**: Hardened compound in The Fringe with its own power generation, water supply, and anti-air capability
- **War Vault**: Classified weapons cache and prototype storage in sub-level compound
- **Phantom Division**: 200-operator black-ops unit, cell-structured, unknown individual identities

### Security Profile
- **Perimeter**: Automated turret rings, seismic sensors, patrol drones at 90-second intervals
- **Interior**: Axiom's own AEGIS AI monitors all internal movement; badges required at every door
- **Black Site Facilities**: Exist in three undisclosed locations across Toronto; suspected in Char Zone North, Digital Quarter sub-basement, and beneath the Docks

### Relationships
- **Nexus Corp**: Supplier relationship (Axiom sells security hardware to Nexus); Axiom occasionally runs extractions for Nexus against mutual enemies
- **Helix Dynamics**: Client — Axiom uses Helix augmentation for Phantom Division operators
- **Vantage Data**: Critical intelligence partner; Axiom feeds AEGIS with Vantage surveillance data
- **OmniCore Energy**: Hostile — the Blackout War of 2039 left lasting enmity; open conflict is avoided but sabotage operations continue

### Vulnerability Intel
- AEGIS has a known exploit (CVE-AX-2045-0091) in its pattern-recognition module — can be fed spoofed thermal signatures to create a "dead zone" in coverage
- Phantom Division communications run on an encrypted channel that Cipher's team has partially cracked; keyword triggers can be intercepted
- Vortmann's personal security detail includes two operators who moonlight for the Iron Veil — conflicting loyalties are a potential leverage point

## 3. Helix Dynamics

**Tagline:** *"Become More."*
**Sector:** Cybernetics, Neural Augmentation, Human Enhancement
**CEO:** Sable Orin-Chase (Age 38, heavily augmented, vocal cords replaced with harmonic synthesizer)
**HQ:** Helix Spire, The Spire District — 67 floors, floors 40-67 restricted

### What They Do
Helix Dynamics made cybernetic augmentation mainstream. Their consumer product line ranges from basic neural comms implants (owned by 60% of Toronto residents) to full skeletal replacement frames. The Helix Credit Program gave millions of low-income residents access to augmentation in exchange for long-term biometric data licensing — critics call it "chrome debt slavery."

Their R&D division, **Apex Division**, develops military-grade augmentations sold exclusively to Axiom Systems and high-net-worth private clients. Rumored projects include neural-override capability (remotely controlling an augmented subject) and a fully synthetic brain substrate called **ECHO**.

### Key Assets
- **ChromeVault**: Encrypted repository of biometric and neural data from 6 million augmented clients
- **Apex Division Lab**: R&D facility on floors 55-67 of Helix Spire; independent network
- **Neural Uplink Grid**: Helix maintains a city-wide uplink network that all Helix implants ping at 15-minute intervals — effectively a surveillance network disguised as a health service
- **ECHO Prototype**: A theoretical fully-synthetic consciousness substrate; two prototypes reportedly exist

### Security Profile
- **Building Access**: Helix uses their own neural-auth system — implanted employees authenticate via implant handshake; no implant, no access above floor 3
- **Counter-Intrusion**: Apex Division uses physical countermeasures including contact-triggered sedative mist systems and mag-lock compartments
- **Known Weakness**: The Neural Uplink Grid can be reverse-exploited — with the right injector, a technician can push firmware updates to nearby Helix implants, including security staff's

### Vulnerability Intel
- Sable Orin-Chase has a personal conflict with Nexus Corp CEO Zhao-Harrington over a stolen research contract — she is known to pay for intel on Nexus
- Three ECHO researchers attempted to whistleblow in 2046; two disappeared, one (Dr. Tomás Vega) is believed to be in Collective protection
- The Neural Uplink Grid's master authentication key is stored on a physical hardware token carried by Helix's CTO, Rin Nakamura

## 4. Vantage Data

**Tagline:** *"Information Is Infrastructure."*
**Sector:** Surveillance, Data Brokerage, Predictive Analytics, Media
**CEO:** Iris Caldwell-Park (Age 44, deliberately minimal augmentation — "authenticity brand")
**HQ:** Vantage Hub, The Digital Quarter — 45 floors, distributed server complex beneath

### What They Do
Vantage Data runs the city's surveillance infrastructure and is the largest data broker in the Toronto Sovereignty Zone. They own 94% of public cameras, all major transit network data feeds, and three of the city's five most-watched media channels. Their **Predictive Index** product scores every resident's behavioral risk profile and sells it to corporate clients, landlords, employers, and — through back channels — other corps' security divisions.

After the 2044 Phantom Breach exposed their dossier collection, Vantage rebranded but didn't change practices. They now call their profiles "Urban Safety Intelligence."

### Key Assets
- **The Lattice**: City-wide surveillance network; 2.3 million cameras, 400,000 microphones, full facial recognition and gait recognition
- **Deep Archive**: Historical behavioral data on every Toronto resident dating to 2029
- **Prediction Engine**: AI that forecasts individual behavior, crowd dynamics, and corporate threat vectors
- **Vantage Media Group**: Controls NewsFlow, Pulse Entertainment, and The Chronicle — three dominant channels

### Security Profile
- **Physical**: Minimal — Vantage relies on information rather than physical force; if you're in the building you've already been tagged, tracked, and an intercept team dispatched
- **Digital**: The most sophisticated intrusion detection in the city; attacks on Vantage infrastructure trigger immediate Axiom-Vantage joint response
- **Known Weakness**: The Lattice has physical blind spots in the Char Zones and deep Underbelly districts; Vantage cameras were destroyed in 2039 and never fully replaced

### Vulnerability Intel
- Caldwell-Park is rumored to maintain a personal "off-books" data cache separate from the corporate archive — location unknown, but believed to be accessible via a private uplink at her Lakeshore Enclave penthouse
- The Deep Archive backup facility is located in Old Town under a defunct library — it's lightly guarded because no one is supposed to know it exists
- Three Lattice maintenance contractors have been turned by the Collective and can create temporary camera blind spots on 4-hour notice

## 5. OmniCore Energy

**Tagline:** *"Power the Future."*
**Sector:** Energy Generation, Grid Management, Zero-Point Research
**CEO:** Petra Volkov-Asante (Age 52, neural-enhanced cognition package)
**HQ:** OmniCore Plex, The Lakeshore Enclave — campus-style, four interconnected buildings

### What They Do
OmniCore Energy controls Toronto's power grid and is the primary developer of zero-point energy technology — a theoretical limitless energy source that, if commercialized, would make OmniCore the most powerful entity on the continent. Their current portfolio includes fusion microreactors that power the corporate towers, a neighborhood-level grid serving 9 districts, and the experimental **Project AURORA** zero-point research facility in The Fringe.

They control the power, which means they have leverage over everyone — and everyone has reason to either cultivate or undermine them.

### Key Assets
- **GridCore**: The master control system for Toronto's power distribution; taking it offline would plunge the city into darkness
- **Project AURORA**: Zero-point energy prototype; if it works, it ends energy scarcity; if it fails catastrophically, it levels a city block
- **Microreactor Network**: 47 fusion microreactors distributed across the city; each is a potential sabotage target
- **Energy Credits**: OmniCore's billing infrastructure means they know exactly which buildings are active and when — passive surveillance capability

### Security Profile
- **GridCore**: Located in a hardened sub-basement of OmniCore Plex; EMP-shielded, independent power, analog override switches
- **Project AURORA**: Remote Fringe location; Axiom-contracted security, heavy drone coverage, radiation hazard protocol means limited human guard presence inside the reactor core
- **Known Weakness**: OmniCore's billing system runs on legacy infrastructure from 2029; it's been patched but not replaced and contains multiple known exploits

### Vulnerability Intel
- Volkov-Asante is the primary obstacle to a rumored Axiom acquisition attempt; she has been in contact with the Collective, suggesting she prefers grid chaos to Axiom control
- Project AURORA's lead scientist, Dr. Emeka Obi, has gone dark for three weeks — either defected, extracted, or dead
- The GridCore analog override switches require physical access but no digital authentication — the security gap is deliberate (digital-only would be vulnerable to Axiom's AEGIS)
