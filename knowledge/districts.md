# Toronto 2047 — City Districts

## Overview

Toronto's metropolitan area is divided into eight primary districts, each with distinct character, power dynamics, and danger levels. The city's original street grid still exists beneath layers of elevated walkways, corporate infrastructure, and jury-rigged additions. Deepline transit connects all districts but passes through corporate checkpoints between major zones.

---

## 1. The Spire

**Control:** Nexus Corp / Helix Dynamics (shared zone)
**Danger Level:** Low (if authorized) / Extreme (if unauthorized)
**Population:** ~200,000 permanent residents; ~600,000 daily workers

### Character
The Spire is Toronto's corporate core — a forest of glass towers connected by elevated skywalks and serviced by private transit pods. Ground level is largely inaccessible; the streets below are utility corridors for service workers and delivery systems. Everything here operates by corporate license. Unauthorized presence triggers immediate security response.

### Key Locations
- **Nexus Tower**: 94-floor biotech headquarters; lower floors are a corporate medical clinic open to Nexus subscribers
- **Helix Spire**: 67-floor augmentation megacorp HQ; ground floor showroom sells consumer chrome
- **The Arcade**: An upscale commercial district between the towers; restaurants, boutiques, entertainment venues for corporate employees; facial recognition required for entry
- **Skybridge Level**: The elevated walkway network connecting all Spire towers; access requires corporate ID or visitor pass
- **The Undercroft**: The service level below street grade; home to maintenance workers, delivery staff, and the invisible labor force that keeps the Spire running

### Mood
Clinical, sterile, quietly threatening. Security is omnipresent but low-profile — embedded cameras, plainclothes Razors, automated systems. The Spire projects an image of clean prosperity while concealing what happens in its sub-levels.

## 2. The Digital Quarter

**Control:** Vantage Data (primary) / Contested (street level)
**Danger Level:** Moderate
**Population:** ~450,000

### Character
The Digital Quarter is Toronto's tech hub — the neighborhood where Vantage Data's sprawling server campus dominates the skyline while hacker dens, black-market data brokers, and rogue tech shops crowd the streets below. It's a district of information warfare where everyone is gathering intelligence on someone else.

### Key Locations
- **Vantage Hub**: 45-floor surveillance megacorp headquarters; server complex extends 8 floors underground
- **The Node Cluster**: A civilian-accessible server hosting facility used by hundreds of businesses; Nexus Corp's backup uplink runs through here — a known infiltration vector
- **Circuit Row**: A three-block stretch of independent tech shops, modders, and grey-market implant dealers
- **The Burrow**: An underground hacker collective meeting space; changes location monthly; known to Cipher and the Phantom Circuit
- **Deadlight**: A bar/club that serves as the Digital Quarter's main social hub for off-corp technologists; encrypted comms available in private booths

### Mood
Paranoid, electric, layered. Everyone here is either watching someone or trying to avoid being watched. Data is currency. Trust is a calculated risk.

## 3. Neon Ward

**Control:** Unclaimed (Iron Veil maintains loose order)
**Danger Level:** Moderate to High
**Population:** ~800,000

### Character
Neon Ward is the city's entertainment district — a riot of competing AR advertisements, music venues, street food markets, illicit augmentation parlors, and pleasure services. Corporate employees come here to spend; the Iron Veil keeps violence organized enough not to scare off the money. The district operates on an unofficial code: commerce first, chaos second.

### Key Locations
- **The Corridor**: The main entertainment strip — 2km of neon, music, and commerce
- **Iron Veil Dispatch**: The mercenary guild's public-facing hiring hall on the Corridor; also serves as a neutral ground where disputes are resolved
- **Black Lotus Market**: The district's premier black market; weapons, chrome, contraband data, stolen corporate assets
- **The Syndicate**: A high-end casino and social club where corp executives, criminals, and freelancers mix; neutral ground by tradition
- **Rust Saints Territory**: The eastern quarter of Neon Ward belongs to the Rust Saints gang; not safe for outsiders without introductions
- **Patch's Former Clinic**: A shuttered medical facility that was Patch's operation before he went freelance; still has useful supply caches hidden in the walls

### Mood
Loud, sensory-overwhelming, transactional. Every interaction here has a price. Nothing is free. Everyone is selling something.

## 4. Lakeshore Enclave

**Control:** OmniCore Energy / Wealthy Private Residents
**Danger Level:** Low (with authorization)
**Population:** ~120,000 (highest per-capita wealth in the city)

### Character
The Lakeshore Enclave occupies the southern waterfront — the only district with consistent access to natural light, clean air (scrubbed by OmniCore's atmospheric processors), and green space. It is home to corporate executives, wealthy independents, and OmniCore's headquarters campus. Security here is discreet but pervasive.

### Key Locations
- **OmniCore Plex**: Four-building campus housing the energy megacorp's operations; GridCore sub-basement is here
- **Caldwell-Park Penthouse**: Vantage CEO's private residence; rumored to house her personal "off-books" data cache
- **Marina District**: Private docks, exclusive restaurants, the kind of place where a crew member in street chrome would be stopped at the door
- **Horizon Academy**: The Enclave's private school; children of every major corporate executive attend here — significant kidnapping risk used in past leverage operations
- **The Green Mile**: A maintained park corridor running the length of the Enclave; one of the only outdoor green spaces in the city

### Mood
Quiet, manicured, and deeply uncomfortable for anyone not wealthy. Security forces are well-paid, professional, and have shoot-first authorizations.

## 5. Old Town

**Control:** Contested (Collective stronghold, corporate patrols limited)
**Danger Level:** Moderate
**Population:** ~300,000

### Character
Old Town is what survives of pre-Corporate Toronto — the original downtown core, largely intact architecturally but economically hollowed out. Corporations don't bother fighting for it; there's nothing profitable here. This neglect makes it the most autonomous district in the city. The Collective is strongest here. Vantage cameras exist but many have been destroyed and not replaced.

### Key Locations
- **Collective Headquarters**: A converted former government building; officially the address of a "community services nonprofit"
- **Vantage Deep Archive Backup**: Hidden beneath the old public library on Yonge Street; minimal security, extreme secrecy
- **The Bazaar**: An informal open-air market operating in a former shopping mall; no corporate surveillance, cash/shard only
- **St. Mike's Sanctuary**: A former hospital repurposed as a free clinic and refugee shelter; Patch maintains contacts here
- **The Archive Bar**: A wood-and-brick bar that predates the Corporate Sovereignty Act; serves as an informal intelligence exchange
- **Ghost's Safehouse**: The crew's primary base of operations; a converted third-floor apartment above a noodle shop

### Mood
Worn, authentic, and quietly defiant. People here have chosen to live outside corporate dependency. The atmosphere is tense but community-oriented.

## 6. The Underbelly

**Control:** Unclaimed (Rust Saints / various micro-gangs)
**Danger Level:** Extreme
**Population:** ~1.2 million (informal; uncounted by corporate census)

### Character
The Underbelly is what happens when corporate governance and city planning both give up on a population. It occupies the lowest levels of the inner city — literal underground sections, basement levels of decommissioned buildings, tunnels, and the spaces between structures. No Deepline service. No corporate clinics. Almost no Vantage surveillance. It runs on shard economy, grey-market augments, and survival.

### Key Locations
- **The Pit**: The Underbelly's largest open-air market; everything is for sale, including human labor
- **Rust Saints Compound**: The gang's fortified headquarters; manufacturing operation for black-market chrome and shards
- **The Catacombs**: A vast tunnel network beneath the Underbelly connecting to Deepline maintenance corridors; used by smugglers and the Collective for covert movement
- **Black Site Rumor**: Multiple sources suggest Axiom Systems operates a covert detention facility somewhere in the deep Underbelly; no confirmed location

### Mood
Raw survival energy. Violence is immediate and consequential. There are no rules except power. The crew comes here when they need to disappear or acquire something that can't be found above ground.

## 7. The Fringe

**Control:** Axiom Systems (northern sector) / OmniCore Energy (southern sector) / Contested (middle)
**Danger Level:** High
**Population:** ~500,000 (industrial workers, displaced residents)

### Character
The Fringe is Toronto's industrial belt — factories, refineries, drone manufacturing facilities, and the sprawling Axiom Citadel compound in the north. The southern sector houses OmniCore's Project AURORA research facility. The middle ground between corporate zones is ungoverned and frequently the site of proxy conflicts.

### Key Locations
- **Axiom Citadel**: Fortified corporate compound; weapons manufacturing, Phantom Division operations, War Vault
- **Project AURORA Site**: OmniCore zero-point energy research facility; industrial complex surrounded by radiation monitoring equipment
- **The Char Zones**: Ruins of the 2039 Blackout War; three former residential blocks completely destroyed; no corporate presence; used by smugglers and the desperate
- **Fringe Markets**: Industrial supply markets where stolen corporate equipment is laundered through legitimate-looking vendors
- **Deepline Maintenance Hub 7**: A key Deepline access point that allows covert movement across multiple districts; known to the crew

### Mood
Industrial, dangerous, and contested. The air tastes of particulates. Corporate security patrols are aggressive. The middle zones smell like burned metal and old blood.

---

## 8. The Docks

**Control:** Silk Network (primary) / OmniCore Energy (port infrastructure)
**Danger Level:** High
**Population:** ~250,000

### Character
The Docks handle what remains of Toronto's legitimate import/export economy, but the Silk Network's information brokerage operation runs through the warehousing and shipping infrastructure. Grey-market goods, corporate contraband, and people who need to disappear all move through the Docks. OmniCore controls the port's power infrastructure, which gives them leverage over everything that happens here.

### Key Locations
- **Silk Network Hub**: A warehouse-front operation on Pier 14; the information broker collective's primary distribution point
- **Nighthawk Terminal**: An unofficial landing pad and staging area for extraction operations
- **The Boneyard**: A decommissioned ship repair facility now used for vehicle modifications, weapons storage, and crew staging
- **Port Authority (Defunct)**: The old government port authority building, now occupied by OmniCore administrative staff; access to port power infrastructure

### Mood
Gray, damp, transactional. The Docks smell like lake water and machine oil. Trust is measured in successful transactions, not words.
