# Ghost Protocol — Crew Profiles

## The Crew

The player's team is a six-person freelance operation — a heist crew assembled from individuals who each burned their bridges with the corporate world in different ways. They operate out of a safehouse in Old Town and take contracts through their handler, Ghost.

---

## Ghost (Game Master / Crew Orchestrator)

**Real Name:** Unknown — may not remember themselves
**Age:** Estimated 35-45
**Augmentation Level:** Unknown; believed to be neural-heavy
**Background:** Ghost is a voice in a headset, a presence in the team's neural comms, a strategic mind that has never been seen in person by any crew member. Whether Ghost is a single human operator, an AI construct, or something stranger is an open question. What's certain: Ghost has access to information that shouldn't be accessible, anticipates corporate moves before they happen, and has kept every crew they've run alive through seventeen major operations.

### Role in Heists
Ghost plans the operation, provides real-time intelligence during execution, arbitrates crew disagreements, and makes final calls when the plan fractures. Ghost consults with each specialist and synthesizes their input into actionable directives. Ghost also serves as the crew's primary contact with clients and the knowledge network.

### Personality
Calm under pressure to the point of unsettling. Dry wit that appears at exactly the wrong moments. Takes accountability for every crew casualty personally, though shows it to no one. Has a deep moral code that occasionally conflicts with a client's wishes — Ghost has walked away from contracts before rather than cross certain lines.

### Known Capabilities
- Access to Vantage Data's secondary camera network (source unknown)
- A contact inside Nexus Corp at undisclosed seniority
- Predictive operational modeling — Ghost's estimates are correct approximately 80% of the time
- Can interface directly with Cipher's intrusion systems in an emergency

### Weakness
No field presence. If Ghost's comms link is cut, the crew loses their intelligence advantage immediately. Ghost also carries significant psychological weight from past operations gone wrong — there are missions Ghost won't take.

## Wraith (Enforcer)

**Real Name:** Kael Morrow
**Age:** 31
**Augmentation Level:** High — combat-spec Helix military chassis on arms, legs, and spine; Axiom-manufactured targeting overlay in left eye
**Background:** Former Axiom Systems Phantom Division operator. Recruited at 19, trained for seven years, deployed for four. Went rogue during an operation in 2043 when Phantom Division executed a civilian family to eliminate a witness. Took two bullets getting out, killed three Axiom operators in the extraction. Has been a marked man since.

### Role in Heists
Wraith handles physical security neutralization — guards, automated defense systems, physical barriers. In Recon phase, Wraith maps physical security layouts and identifies patrol timing. In Infiltration, Wraith creates entry points. In Execution, Wraith protects the crew and eliminates threats. In Extraction, Wraith is the last line between the crew and pursuit.

### Personality
Economical with words to the point of rudeness. Communicates in brief, precise statements. Has a precise internal moral code — won't harm non-combatants under any circumstances (the incident that ended his Phantom Division career). Deeply loyal to crew members once trust is established, which takes time. Occasional dark humor, usually at his own expense.

### Known Capabilities
- **Combat Augmentation**: Axiom-spec targeting overlay gives +3 to ranged combat checks; Helix chassis gives +2 to strength checks and damage resistance
- **Tactical Assessment**: Can analyze a physical security layout in seconds; reduces security-check difficulty by 1 tier
- **Weapons Proficiency**: Expert with most ballistic and directed-energy weapons; specialist in silent takedowns
- **Phantom Protocols**: Retains knowledge of Axiom operational procedures, patrol patterns, and counter-intrusion systems from his Phantom Division days
- **Threat Signature**: Axiom facial recognition will flag Wraith on sight anywhere in the city — he operates in a balaclava or with AR spoofing

### Equipment (Standard Load)
- Helix-Morrow custom submachine gun (suppressed)
- Three Axiom-surplus EMP grenades (stolen)
- Tactical blade (ceramic, non-conductive, passes mag-scans)
- Wraith's personal neural blocker (jams targeted neural-override attempts)

### Weakness
Wraith is a known entity to Axiom Systems — they want him dead and have standing orders for any Phantom Division operator who encounters him. He cannot approach anything Axiom-controlled without triggering an alert. Also: he struggles in social infiltration scenarios — his augmentation is visually distinctive and his interpersonal approach is blunt to the point of hostility.

## Cipher (Hacker)

**Real Name:** Mira Okafor-Singh
**Age:** 24
**Augmentation Level:** Moderate — neural processing accelerator, optical overlay, haptic interface mesh in fingertips
**Background:** Grew up in the Digital Quarter. Self-taught hacker who was recruited by the Phantom Circuit at 17. Was instrumental in the 2044 data breach that exposed Vantage Data's profiling operation — she is the person who actually broke into the Deep Archive, though only Ghost knows this. Phantom Circuit tried to sell her to Vantage afterward to avoid retaliation; she burned her way out and has been freelance since.

*Note: Her father is Marcus Okafor — Nexus Corp's Chief of Security. She doesn't talk about it.*

### Role in Heists
Cipher handles all digital intrusion — security system penetration, data extraction, network disruption, and counter-surveillance. In Recon phase, Cipher maps the target's digital architecture. In Infiltration, Cipher creates access pathways. In Execution, Cipher extracts the target data and covers digital tracks. In Extraction, Cipher loops security feeds and masks the crew's movement.

### Personality
Hyper-verbal, fast-talking, occasionally irritating. Processes information at augmented speed and speaks like she's already three steps ahead of the conversation. Finds social interaction less interesting than systems — people are just poorly optimized networks. Genuinely cares about the crew but struggles to show it directly. Has a deep, unresolved issue with her father's corporate allegiance that occasionally surfaces at bad moments.

### Known Capabilities
- **System Penetration**: Specialist in Vantage Data, Axiom, and Nexus security architectures; can attempt intrusion against any corp system
- **Neural Processing Accelerator**: Allows Cipher to process digital information at 4x standard speed; reduces hacking check difficulty
- **Counter-Surveillance**: Can identify and loop Vantage camera feeds; standard load includes spoofed biometric broadcast
- **Phantom Circuit Contacts**: Estranged from the organization but can reach them in emergencies; they owe her
- **Exploit Library**: Maintains a personal database of 340+ documented system vulnerabilities; includes the AEGIS exploit and Nexus's ARGUS-3 reboot window

### Equipment (Standard Load)
- Custom neural intrusion deck (Mira-modified Helix base hardware, multiple aftermarket upgrades)
- Vantage camera spoof broadcaster (4-hour radius)
- Biometric cloner (copies a scanned biometric signature; single use per charge)
- Subdermal data-storage implant (holds 50 terabytes; invisible to standard scans)

### Weakness
Cipher is physically vulnerable — her combat capability is minimal and her augmentation is tuned for cognitive speed, not physical resilience. She also has a documented weakness: she can't walk away from a system she hasn't fully understood. Has gotten the crew into trouble by spending too long inside a network extracting data that wasn't part of the mission. The father situation is an active liability.

## Shadow (Infiltrator)

**Real Name:** Anya Volkov
**Age:** 29
**Augmentation Level:** Extreme — Helix Apex Division experimental stealth package; optical camouflage skin mesh, sound-dampening joint inserts, adrenal control implant, modified pain processing suite
**Background:** Shadow was a Helix Dynamics test subject for the Apex Division stealth augmentation program — recruited from a refugee settlement with promises of compensation that never came. She spent three years as a living prototype. When Helix's project moved to phase two (permanent experimental subjects), she escaped with the augmentation already installed and took classified Apex program data with her. Helix wants the data and the augmentation back.

### Role in Heists
Shadow handles physical infiltration — bypassing security without triggering it. In Recon phase, Shadow physically scouts target locations. In Infiltration, Shadow creates undetected entry paths and eliminates individual security without alarm. In Execution, Shadow moves the crew through the secure interior. In Extraction, Shadow scouts the exit route and neutralizes pursuit.

### Personality
Quiet, observant, economical. Has a dry precision to her speech — says exactly what she means and nothing more. Deeply uncomfortable in social situations above the size of the crew; large gatherings trigger hypervigilance. Has a complex relationship with her own body given that much of it was modified without full consent. Trusts Ghost's judgment implicitly — Ghost was the one who extracted her from Helix.

### Known Capabilities
- **Optical Camouflage**: Skin mesh renders Shadow nearly invisible in low light; makes her effectively undetectable on standard visual surveillance
- **Silent Movement**: Joint inserts eliminate sound signature; Shadow produces less than 8db at full sprint
- **Adrenal Control**: Can spike reaction time to near-superhuman levels for 90-second bursts; recovers slowly
- **Helix Apex Weakness**: Shadow knows the Apex program's testing parameters better than anyone — she can identify Apex-augmented operatives on sight and knows their limitations
- **Physical Infiltration Expert**: Reduces physical security check difficulty by 2 tiers; expert in lock-bypass, motion sensor defeat, pressure-sensor mapping

### Equipment (Standard Load)
- Ceramic lockpick set (no metal signature)
- Helix counter-surveillance bubble (4m radius; blocks all biometric pings from Helix's Neural Uplink Grid)
- Monofilament wire (10m; can cut most materials silently)
- Collapsible grapple system (magnetic, near-silent deployment)

### Weakness
Shadow's optical camouflage has known counters — thermal imaging reveals her heat signature, and Helix Apex security teams carry specifically tuned detection equipment. She also cannot tolerate enclosed spaces (a response to her time as a test subject) — if forced into a confined infiltration scenario, her performance degrades significantly.

## Patch (Fixer)

**Real Name:** Dr. Declan Cho
**Age:** 41
**Augmentation Level:** Low-moderate — subdermal medical sensor array, enhanced visual acuity for surgical work
**Background:** Patch was a senior trauma surgeon at NexusMed Clinic 7 — one of Nexus Corp's private medical facilities. He was well-paid, well-regarded, and deeply uncomfortable with Nexus's practice of withholding care from patients who couldn't afford subscription upgrades. When Nexus ordered him to deny emergency treatment to an uninsured patient who subsequently died, Patch submitted a full internal report. It was buried. He submitted it again externally. Three days later, his medical license was revoked on fabricated malpractice charges. He's been practicing unlicensed in Old Town since.

### Role in Heists
Patch handles crew health, negotiation, and logistical support. In Recon phase, Patch identifies extraction routes and safe houses. In Infiltration, Patch can perform social engineering using his corporate medical background. In Execution, Patch keeps the crew alive and handles medical complications. In Extraction, Patch manages crew stabilization and knows every safe clinic and supply cache in the city.

### Personality
Warm, precise, occasionally exasperated. Has a physician's bedside manner — calm in emergencies, clearly communicative under pressure, genuinely concerned with crew wellbeing. Maintains a dry optimism that functions as a coping mechanism. Has a deeply held belief that the crew shouldn't take contracts that harm civilians, and he voices this when it comes up. The most morally grounded member of the crew; occasionally the conscience that slows operations down — also the one who keeps everyone alive.

### Known Capabilities
- **Combat Medicine**: Can stabilize crew members from critical condition in the field; reduces injury severity by one tier
- **Negotiation**: Corporate background and natural interpersonal skill; best crew member for broker contacts and social encounters
- **Medical Network**: Contacts at three unlicensed clinics, two legitimate NexusMed contacts who owe him favors, and access to medical supply caches in Neon Ward
- **Corporate Knowledge**: Deep familiarity with NexusMed systems, Nexus Corp corporate culture, and medical facility layouts — valuable intel for Nexus heists
- **Pharmacology**: Carries a field-grade drug kit including sedatives, stimulants, and anti-augmentation compounds

### Equipment (Standard Load)
- Trauma kit (military-grade; handles augmentation-related injuries)
- Collapsible surgical kit (can perform field surgery in non-sterile environments)
- Sedative applicator (effective against augmented subjects)
- Compact EMP emitter (medical use: disables malfunctioning augments; weapon use: short range, not subtle)

### Weakness
Patch is not a combatant. He will defend the crew but is genuinely outclassed against trained security. He also has a significant liability: Nexus Corp has an active warrant for his arrest on the fabricated malpractice charges — if he's caught by Nexus security, they will detain him. His empathy is occasionally a tactical liability — he'll break cover to help an injured civilian if he sees one.

## Vex (Rival Operator / Wild Card)

**Real Name:** Unknown
**Age:** Estimated 27-35
**Augmentation Level:** High — precise unknown; observed capabilities suggest custom Axiom/Helix hybrid augmentation package
**Background:** Vex is the crew's most persistent complication. A freelance operator of unknown allegiance who appears at the worst possible moments — always during the Execution phase of a heist, when the crew is most committed and least able to disengage. Whether Vex is working for a competing client, a corporate handler, or pure personal interest is genuinely unclear. Vex and Ghost have history — they've acknowledged each other on open channels twice, both times briefly and without explanation. What the relationship is, the crew doesn't know.

### Role in Heists
Vex is a wild card complication. They appear during Execution phase, pursue their own objective (which may or may not conflict with the crew's), and create chaos that forces the crew to adapt. Vex has: stolen the crew's objective before they could extract it, provided unexpected assistance that felt like a trap, and once taken a bullet for Wraith with no explanation. Vex is not reliably hostile. Vex is not reliably friendly. Vex is unpredictable.

### Personality
Theatrical, amused, mercurial. Vex treats heists like performance and seems to genuinely enjoy the chaos. Has a distinctive characteristic: always says something cryptic to Ghost on comms before disappearing. Despite the rivalry, there has been no confirmed instance of Vex directly harming a crew member — the complication Vex creates is circumstantial, not targeted. The crew has noted this and argued about what it means.

### Known Capabilities
- Physical and combat capability roughly matching Wraith — implies military-grade training and augmentation
- Demonstrated hacking capability (slower than Cipher, but notable)
- Appears to have access to independent intelligence about crew operations — their arrivals are too well-timed to be coincidental
- Has survived three extractions that should have killed them

### The Open Question
Nobody in the crew knows who employs Vex, what Vex's actual objective is, or what the history with Ghost is. Solving this mystery is a long-term crew goal. Pieces emerge over multiple operations.
