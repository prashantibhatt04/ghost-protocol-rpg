# Toronto 2047 — Factions and Power Brokers

## Overview

Beyond the five megacorporations, Toronto 2047 is shaped by independent factions with their own resources, agendas, and relationship with the corporate order. Freelance crews navigate relationships with these factions constantly — some are employers, some are obstacles, some are both.

---

## 1. The Collective

**Type:** Anti-corporate resistance organization
**Territory:** Old Town (primary); cells in every district
**Size:** ~8,000 active members; ~50,000 sympathizers
**Leader:** Known only as "The Architect" — identity unknown even to most Collective members; believed to be a former corporate executive

### What They Do
The Collective is the most organized anti-corporate resistance in Toronto. They provide social services in ungoverned districts (food, medical care, legal aid), run a counter-surveillance network, protect whistleblowers and defectors from corporate retaliation, and conduct targeted operations against corporate infrastructure — data exposure, sabotage, extraction.

They are not terrorists: The Collective's operational code explicitly prohibits civilian casualties and targets corporate assets, not people. This makes them more sympathetic to the general population and more frustrating to the corporations who want to label them as a threat.

### Relationship with the Crew
The Collective has contracted the crew on three previous operations. Patch has personal ties to their Old Town operations. Ghost has an unknown relationship with The Architect. The Collective is a reliable if low-paying client that trades in favors as much as shards.

### Assets
- Collective HQ in Old Town
- Three Lattice maintenance contractors who can blind Vantage cameras
- Dr. Tomás Vega (Helix whistleblower) in protective custody
- Underground clinic and shelter network
- Communication infrastructure that operates outside Vantage's surveillance

### Wants
- The Predictive Index list destroyed (Operation PHANTOM BREACH 2)
- Corporate whistleblowers protected
- Knowledge that the corporations have violated human rights surfaced publicly
- Ultimately: renegotiation of the Corporate Sovereignty Act

## 2. The Iron Veil

**Type:** Mercenary guild
**Territory:** Neon Ward (primary); dispatch presence in all districts
**Size:** ~1,500 active contractors; ~400 support staff
**Leader:** Commander Brix Okafor (no relation to Marcus Okafor; the shared name is coincidence that has caused confusion twice)

### What They Do
The Iron Veil is the premier mercenary organization in Toronto — a guild structure that vets contractors, brokers contracts, maintains neutral ground in Neon Ward, and takes a 15% cut of all brokered work. They enforce a code among their contractors: no civilian casualties, no breaking contracts, pay your tithe. Violations result in expulsion and, for serious breaches, active hunting.

They are not politically aligned — they work for corporations, factions, and freelancers equally. Two of Axiom CEO Vortmann's personal security detail moonlight for the Iron Veil. The crew has done work through Iron Veil dispatch before.

### Relationship with the Crew
The crew has a standing relationship with Iron Veil Dispatch. The Iron Veil provides contract introduction services, neutral ground for crew meetings, and occasional contract work. Wraith's history with Axiom makes some Iron Veil contractors nervous — Axiom is a major client.

### Assets
- Iron Veil Dispatch (hiring hall in Neon Ward)
- The Syndicate (neutral ground casino)
- Network of 1,500 vetted contractors across all skill types
- Intelligence on active corporate security deployments (from contractors embedded in corp security)

### Wants
- Contracts honored; reputation maintained
- No inter-faction wars in their territory
- The crew to stop bringing Axiom-level heat into Neon Ward

## 3. The Silk Network

**Type:** Information brokerage and smuggling network
**Territory:** The Docks (primary); distributed nodes everywhere
**Size:** Unknown — Silk Network membership is kept entirely confidential
**Leader:** "The Weaver" — rumored to be a woman operating from a ship in Lake Ontario; identity unknown

### What They Do
The Silk Network is the city's information broker collective — they buy, sell, and trade intelligence on everyone and everything. Corporate executives, crew operations, faction movements, government contacts — all of it passes through the Network eventually. They also run the most sophisticated physical smuggling operation in Toronto, using Dock infrastructure to move people, goods, and data out of the city or between districts.

Unlike the Iron Veil, the Silk Network is covert by nature — membership isn't announced. You're approached, not recruited. Marcus Okafor (Nexus Chief of Security) has gambling debts with the Network — a leverage point the crew knows about.

### Relationship with the Crew
Ghost has a direct contact inside the Silk Network — one of the few people who can reach The Weaver's associates. The Silk Network has served as the conduit for at least two crew contracts, including the initial approach for Operation GENESIS. They are trusted brokers but charge premium rates for information.

### Assets
- Pier 14 warehouse hub in the Docks
- Nighthawk Terminal (covert extraction staging)
- Intelligence files on every major player in Toronto
- Physical smuggling network with city-wide reach
- Marcus Okafor (leverage asset)

### Wants
- Their operational secrecy maintained
- Debts collected
- The crew to never disclose their role in any contract chain

## 4. The Rust Saints

**Type:** Street gang / black-market manufacturer
**Territory:** Eastern Neon Ward; pockets in the Underbelly
**Size:** ~400 active members
**Leader:** Taja Renner, known as "Saint" (Age 34, heavy Helix grey-market augmentation, right arm fully replaced)

### What They Do
The Rust Saints are the dominant street gang in the Neon Ward's eastern quarter. They manufacture and distribute grey-market Helix-compatible augmentation, counterfeit shards, and black-market weapons. They are rough, territorial, and deeply hostile to corporate incursion — three Rust Saints were killed in Axiom operations in 2043, which means they hate Axiom with genuine intensity.

They are not sophisticated operators but they are well-connected at street level. They know everything that moves through eastern Neon Ward.

### Relationship with the Crew
Wraith has street-level contacts among the Saints from his early post-Axiom years. Shadow has twice used their Underbelly connections for safe passage. The Saints tolerate the crew because the crew doesn't operate in their territory without permission and pays fair rates. Any operation that requires Underbelly access will likely need Rust Saints facilitation.

### Assets
- Extensive Underbelly tunnel knowledge
- Black-market chrome and weapons supply
- Deep street-level intelligence in Neon Ward
- Hostility toward Axiom that can be activated

### Wants
- Axiom kept out of their territory
- Payment for services in shards, not credits
- The crew not bringing Helix operatives into their district (Shadow is a complication here)

## 5. Phantom Circuit

**Type:** Elite hacker collective
**Territory:** Digital Quarter (distributed; no physical HQ)
**Size:** ~60 core members; ~200 associated
**Leader:** "Null" — identity unknown; believed to be augmented with a full neural interface that allows direct communication with networked systems

### What They Do
Phantom Circuit is the Digital Quarter's elite hacker collective — the organization that, in public mythology, executed the 2044 Phantom Breach. In reality, Cipher did the actual penetration work. The Circuit took credit, accepted the resulting fame, then tried to sell Cipher to Vantage to avoid retaliation. Their reputation exceeds their ethics.

Despite this, they remain the most skilled hacking collective in Toronto with significant digital resources: zero-day exploits, corporate network maps, backdoor access to numerous systems, and a city-wide network of deployed intrusion tools.

### Relationship with the Crew
Cipher's relationship with the Circuit is estranged and complicated. She is simultaneously owed an enormous debt (they stole her work) and holds a kind of professional contempt that the Circuit's current members find intimidating — she's better than any of them and they know it. The Circuit will cooperate with the crew when their interests align but cannot be fully trusted.

### Assets
- Zero-day exploit library (240+ documented)
- Corporate network maps (Vantage, Helix, partial Axiom)
- Deployed backdoors in 12 Digital Quarter server nodes
- Null's unusual direct-network interface capability

### Wants
- To rehabilitate their reputation after it became known (in crew circles) that they tried to sell Cipher
- Cipher's forgiveness, or at least neutrality
- Their own operations not disrupted

## 6. Corporate Security Forces

Each megacorp maintains its own security apparatus. Understanding the distinctions matters for operational planning.

### Nexus Razors
- Jurisdiction: Nexus Corp facilities and the Spire zone
- Profile: Ex-military, Helix-augmented, professionally trained
- Standard load: Axiom-manufactured directed-energy sidearms, light body armor
- Response time: 4 minutes within Spire; 12 minutes city-wide
- Communication: Nexus-encrypted frequency, monitored by ARGUS-3

### Axiom Phantom Division
- Jurisdiction: City-wide; primary in Fringe and Digital Quarter
- Profile: Black-ops specialists; Axiom/Helix hybrid augmentation; cell-structured — individual operators don't know each other
- Standard load: Full combat spec; classified hardware
- Response time: Variable by cell; can deploy in under 2 minutes with pre-positioning
- Communication: Partially cracked by Cipher (keyword interception only)
- Note: Wraith is a known former member — any Phantom Division contact triggers termination protocols

### Helix Apex Security
- Jurisdiction: Helix facilities; follow Helix assets when deployed
- Profile: Test subjects turned operators; experimental augmentation; specifically configured to detect Shadow's augmentation signature
- Note: Apex security is the crew's most personal threat — they want Shadow back

### Vantage Watch
- Jurisdiction: Digital Quarter and Lattice network citywide
- Profile: Low physical capability; high intelligence capability; function as surveillance and rapid-alert rather than combat force
- Note: If Vantage Watch tags you, Axiom Phantom Division is dispatched as enforcement

### OmniCore GridWatch
- Jurisdiction: OmniCore facilities, Lakeshore Enclave, Fringe southern sector
- Profile: Standard security; Axiom-contracted; no distinguishing augmentation
- Note: Least threatening of the five; most cooperative if approached with plausible corporate cover
